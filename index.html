T-CACHE: Fast Inference for Masked Generative Transformer-Based TTS via Prompt-Aware Feature Caching

Abstract

Masked generative transformers (MGTs) have achieved remarkable performance in text-to-speech (TTS), enabling high-quality and parallel speech generation. However, their iterative inference remains computationally expensive, often requiring dozens of decoding steps. To address this, we propose T-Cache, a training-free caching mechanism that accelerates MGT-based TTS by reusing redundant features across adjacent decoding steps. Furthermore, we investigate the role of classifier-free guidance (CFG) across textto-semantic (T2S) and semantic-to-acoustic (S2A) stages, demonstrating that it can be bypassed in S2A without significant quality loss. We also show that prompt tokens exhibit strong similarity across steps and can be cached to further reduce computation. Experiments show that T-Cache achieves up to 2.5-3.41× speedup while maintaining comparable performance in speech robustness and prosody, with improved naturalness.
